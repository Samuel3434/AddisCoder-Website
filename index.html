Addis Coder

AddisCoder is a free intensive 4-week summer program in Addis Ababa, Ethiopia introducing high schoolers to programming and algorithms. The program ran previously in 2011, 2016, 2018, 2019, and 2023. The next offering of AddisCoder will be July 14 to August 9, 2024..

  • Program Overview

    The AddisCoder program was founded in Summer 2011 and has since been dedicated to providing free training in algorithms and computer science to high school students from across Ethiopia. This unique initiative is designed to offer a comprehensive learning experience, combining theoretical knowledge with practical skills in a residential setting. Participants benefit from an all-inclusive program where housing, meals, transportation, and registration are 100% free, thanks to generous donations, sponsorships, and partnerships. The program lasts for 4 weeks and is currently organized by AddisCoder, Inc. in close collaboration with the Ethiopian Ministry of Education.

  • Curriculum

    Introduction to Computer Science

    AddisCoder introduces participants to the field of computer science, laying a strong foundation in the basics of Python programming. The curriculum is designed to ensure that students not only learn to code but also understand the underlying principles of computer science.

    Advanced Topics

    • Order of Growth
    • Recurrences
    • Induction
    • Memoization
    • Greedy Algorithms
    • Sorting
    • Numerical Algorithms

  • Hands-on Learning

    The course is very hands-on: class meets for 8-8.5 hours per day, including a lunch break in the middle, with roughly 2 hours per day devoted to lecture. The rest of a class day is devoted to hands-on lab exercises in which students solve problems by writing code.

History

2011

Founding and First Edition

Spring 2011: Prof. Jelani Nelson founded AddisCoder while finishing his Ph.D. at MIT, aiming to bring computer science education to Ethiopian high school students.
July 2011: The first program was held at Addis Ababa Institute of Technology, with 82 students, mostly from private schools, completing the course. The program received support from local media and volunteers..

2016

Expanding Inclusivity

2016: To address the lack of public school students in the earlier cohort, AddisCoder partnered with the Meles Zenawi Foundation (MZF). This partnership helped involve top public school students from across Ethiopia, selected with the Ministry of Education’s assistance.
Structural Changes: AddisCoder Inc. focused on assembling teaching staff and curriculum development, while MZF managed logistics, including student selection, travel, and accommodation.

2019

Significant Growth and Regional Impact

Summer 2019: AddisCoder expanded to include over 170 high school students, with 40-45% female participation. The program also saw the involvement of 17 Ethiopian teaching assistants and a global teaching staff.
Regional Influence: Inspired by AddisCoder, MZF developed new programs for younger students in other Ethiopian cities, helping prepare them for future participation in AddisCoder.

2023

Continued Expansion and Recent Developments

2023: AddisCoder continued to expand, maintaining its focus on inclusivity and diversity across Ethiopia. The latest iteration involved participants from diverse backgrounds, with ongoing efforts to provide more opportunities for students nationwide. More details on the 2023 program are forthcoming.
